Professional Life

I am still actively playing bassoon and have a high standard of my own playing. I am a professional bassoonist, saxophonist, and teacher. I have been teaching bassoon and saxophone for just over 10 years. I am currently a sub for the Fort Collins Symphony, and have performed and been invited to perform with other professional symphonies such as the Norman Philharmonic and the Oklahoma City Repertory Theater. I am certified to teach K-12 music and early childhood music.

As I work full-time as a support technician for Alchemer, I do not always have the privelege to practice every single day, but I make sure to keep myself engaged in music whether I am playing bassoon or not.

My Approach

My approach to teaching music is to begin by focusing on good embouchure, rhythm, and technique. When beginning with newer students I primarily will focus on getting a proper sound out of the instrument which entails embouchure practice. Afterwards, I make sure that my students are playing as much music as they can. I am very intentional about assigning etudes that are within my students capabilities but not terribly easy! Technique and rhythm are often built through excessive playing and then we can begin thinking about tone, vibrato, and theory.

During the 2nd or 3rd years of playing I will begin talking about reed making or reed maintenance along with our studies of vibrato and tone production. This often helps students understand that they can have a big part in their own tone production through reed maintenance.
